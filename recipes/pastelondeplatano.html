Pastelon de Platano Maduro

Description

A casserole that combines ripe plantains, melting cheese and beef filling

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoon olive oil, (or vegetable oil)
  • 1 red onion, minced
  • 1 tablespoon mashed garlic, (3 large garlic cloves)
  • 1 pound ground beef,
  • 1 cup tomato sauce,
  • 1 green bell pepper, or red, seeded and minced
  • 1½ teaspoons salt, (or more, to taste)
  • ½ teaspoons black pepper (freshly-cracked, or ground), (or more, to taste)
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh cilantro, (or parsley)
  • 6 plantain (ripe, yellow)
  • ¼ cup salted butter, plus extra to grease pan.
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese

Steps

  1. To make Filling

    1. Cook meat

      In a large skillet, heat oil over medium-low heat. Stir in onion and cook and stir until it turns translucent. Add garlic and cook and stir for a minute. Add minced meat and cook and stir, breaking it into small clumps until it browns.

    2. Season

      Pour in tomato sauce. Add bell pepper, salt, and pepper. Stir and cover. Simmer for 3 minutes.

    3. Add extra ingredient

      Uncover and mix in an optional ingredient of your choice, or none at all if you so prefer. Taste and season with salt and pepper to taste if needed. Remove from the heat.

  2. Peel the plantains

    3. Peel the plantains and boil adding 1 teaspoon of salt to the water. Once the plantains are fork tender (15 - 20 mins), remove them from the heat.

  3. Mash the plantains

    4. Drain the water right away, and mash them with a fork. Add the butter and keep mashing until it is very smooth with no lumps.

  4. Layer

    5. Grease a 1-inch tall baking pan. Put half of the plantain mixture in the baking pan. Cover with half of the cheese. Cover the cheese with the meat. Cover with the remaining plantain mixture. Cover with the rest of the cheese.

  5. Bake

    6. Cook in preheated oven to 350 °F [175 °C] until the top is golden brown.

  6. Serving

    7. It will be easier to serve if you wait five minutes after removing it from the oven.
